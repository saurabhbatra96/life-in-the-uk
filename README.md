# Life in the UK — Study App

Summaries + flashcards built from *Life in the United Kingdom: A guide for new residents, 3rd Edition* (the PDF you provided).

## How to use

Open `index.html` in any browser — no install, no server, works offline. Progress is saved in your browser (localStorage), so use the same browser to keep it.

- **📖 Summaries** — every section of all 5 chapters, condensed. Read these first.
- **🃏 Flashcards** — 854 cards, each tagged with its source page in the PDF. Flip with **Space**, navigate with **←/→**, grade with **1** (still learning) / **2** (know it). Filter by chapter, or tick "only cards I don't know" for revision loops.
- **✅ Checklists** — the book's own "Check that you understand" boxes. The book warns these alone aren't enough to pass; they're the floor, not the ceiling.
- **⏳ Timeline** — every dated fact from the flashcards laid out as a vertical timeline, from 55 BC to the present, grouped by century. Built automatically from the cards (years, decades like "1920s", and "18th century"-style dates), so a card appears under each date it mentions. Ideal for chapter 3, where the book says to know *all dates*.
- **📊 Progress** — per-chapter mastery. The real test: 24 questions from anywhere in the book, 75% to pass.

## What the book itself says to focus on (built into the card selection)

- **Chapter 3 (history):** know *all dates*, *all people*, *all Acts of Parliament*; understand monarchy vs Parliament, Protestants vs Catholics, Britain vs the colonies, and how the right to vote developed.
- **Chapter 2:** differences between the UK, Great Britain, and the Crown dependencies.
- Questions come from the *whole* book, including the introduction (chapter 1 covers the test rules themselves).

## Fact provenance

- Every card records the PDF page its answer comes from.
- All 854 cards were written strictly from the extracted book text, then independently re-audited card-by-card against the text (dates digit-by-digit).
- The **"Key facts" deck (⭐)** comes from an appendix added by the PDF's publisher (Britizen — not part of the official handbook). It contained 12 errors contradicting the handbook's own chapters (e.g. Boer War "1889-1902" instead of 1899–1902). Those cards were corrected to match the handbook text and carry a note showing the misprint.
- Known typos in this PDF edition vs the official print (cards follow the PDF, as it was the requested source): Handel's dates printed as "1695–1759" (official: 1685–1759), "Balthazar's Feast" (official: *Belshazzar's Feast*), "Jensen Button" (official: Jenson Button).
